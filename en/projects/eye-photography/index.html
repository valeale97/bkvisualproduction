About

What is eye photography?

Your iris as personal artwork

Eye photography is a close-up macro portrait of the iris - the coloured part of your eye. Every iris has its own structure, colour transitions, texture and fine details, so the final image becomes a personal art piece rather than a standard portrait.

During the session we use controlled lighting and macro photography equipment to capture the iris clearly, without touching the eye. After the photo is taken, the image is carefully optimised for clarity, contrast and colour, then prepared as a digital file, print or framed piece.

The experience is designed for individuals, couples, friends and families who want a unique keepsake or personalised wall art.

FAQ

Questions and answers

What exactly is iris / eye photography?

Iris photography is a close-up macro photo of the coloured part of your eye. The final result is edited as artwork, not as a medical scan. We focus on the natural colours, fibres, rings and small details that usually cannot be seen clearly in a normal portrait.

Is anything put in my eye?

No. The session is non-contact. Nothing is placed in the eye and there are no drops. You only sit still, keep the eye open for a short moment and look toward the camera.

Is the light safe and does it hurt?

The session is painless and uses controlled light for a very short time. If you have strong light sensitivity, epilepsy, a recent eye operation or a specific eye condition, please tell us before booking and check with your doctor if you are unsure.

From what age can children be photographed?

For best results, iris photography is recommended from around 5 years old, because the child needs to sit still and look toward the lens for a few moments. Younger children are usually not recommended unless they can calmly cooperate. A parent or guardian must be present.

Can babies or very small children be photographed?

In most cases, no. The problem is not safety, but movement and focus. Macro photography needs the eye to stay very still; if the child moves too much, the image may not be sharp enough to deliver a good final artwork.

Can pets be photographed?

At the moment, pets are not photographed for this service. The setup is planned for people and requires calm positioning, stable eye direction and controlled lighting, so we currently offer iris photography only for human clients.

Can I wear glasses or contact lenses?

Glasses need to be removed. Soft daily lenses can sometimes stay in, but for the cleanest result it is better to remove them if possible. Coloured, reflective, hard or monthly lenses should be removed because they can create reflections, texture changes or distortion.

Do I need to prepare before the session?

No special preparation is required. For the best result, come rested, avoid very red or irritated eyes, and avoid heavy eye makeup or glitter around the eye because it can reflect light. Bring lens case/fluid if you plan to remove contact lenses.

How long does the session take?

The actual photo is quick, often only a few minutes per person. Extra time may be needed for children, couples, families, choosing the best image, or preparing a combined layout.

Do brown or very dark eyes work?

Yes. Dark eyes can look very interesting in iris photography because the macro light and editing reveal texture, fibres and colour details that are normally hard to see in everyday light.

What do I receive after the session?

You receive the selected edited iris artwork as a digital file. Depending on your choice, we can also prepare fine art prints, framed pictures, larger wall pieces or additional custom edits.

Can two or more irises be combined in one artwork?

Yes. Duo, family and group compositions can be created with multiple irises in one layout. This is popular for couples, siblings, parents and children, friends, or gift pieces.

Can I order prints later?

Yes. Additional prints, framed versions and larger wall formats can be arranged later. It is best to order while the edited files are still available in the project archive.
